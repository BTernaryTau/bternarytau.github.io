Comparison to the mixed factorial

I came up with the mu factorial before I was aware of the mixed factorial, but the name â€œmu factorialâ€ came after I researched other factorial variants and learned about the very similar mixed factorial. I tried to choose the details of the mu factorial to be as natural as possible in the context of googology. The base case 0Î¼!=00\mu! = 00Î¼!=0 was chosen so that the value of nÎ¼!n\mu!nÎ¼! would be generated from the previous value, the nnnth hyper operator, and nnn itself. I also chose to have the previous value serve as the hyperexponent since this generally leads to larger values compared to using it as the base.

Notably, these design choices donâ€™t really affect the growth rate since both factorial variants diagonalize over the hyper operators. In fact, it can be shown that the mu factorial grows at the same rate as the mixed factorial, though the mu factorial does dominate the mixed factorial for all arguments greater than 3.
