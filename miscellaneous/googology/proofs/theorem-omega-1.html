Theorem ω\omegaω-1

This theorem establishes a simple upper bound on the Wainer hierarchy up to ω\omegaω in terms of hyper operators.

2⋅fα(n)≤2[α+1](2⋅n) for α<ω2⋅fω(n)≤2[n+1](2⋅n)\begin{align*} 2 \cdot f_\alpha(n) &\le 2 [\alpha + 1] (2 \cdot n) \text{ for } \alpha < \omega \\ 2 \cdot f_\omega(n) &\le 2 [n + 1] (2 \cdot n) \\ \end{align*}2⋅fα​(n)2⋅fω​(n)​≤2[α+1](2⋅n) for α<ω≤2[n+1](2⋅n)​

The correctness of this bound was originally proven by Deedlit for 1≤α<ω1 \le \alpha < \omega1≤α<ω and n≥1n \ge 1n≥1. This proof follows the same approach as Deedlit’s proof. It is worth noting that Deedlit has also proven the correctness of a more complicated but tighter upper bound.
