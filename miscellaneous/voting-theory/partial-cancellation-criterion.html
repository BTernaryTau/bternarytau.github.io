Partial cancellation criterion

The partial cancellation criterion requires that every portion of a vote must have an opposite which cancels it out. Specifically, for every valid ballot and subset of the candidates there must exist at least one other ballot that represents the opposite set of preferences for those candidates, and adding these two ballots to an election must never change the result from one candidate in the subset to another.

Note that this definition assumes that mmm passes the anonymity criterion.

This criterion is intended to be a strengthened version of the opposite cancellation criterion. While the opposite cancellation criterion applies only to full ballots, the partial cancellation criterion extends its requirements to partial ballots as well.

The partial cancellation criterion implies both the opposite cancellation criterion and the cancellation criterion.
